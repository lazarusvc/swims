# Managing Notification Preferences

You can control exactly how SWIMS notifies you — per notification type and per channel.

## Opening Preferences

Navigate to **Account → Notification Preferences**, or click the link at the bottom of the notification bell dropdown.

## The Preferences Page

The preferences page lists each notification type you have received recently, with toggle switches for each delivery channel:

| Toggle | Controls |
|--------|---------|
| **In-App** | Whether the notification appears in the bell dropdown |
| **Email** | Whether an individual email is sent immediately |
| **Digest** | Whether it is included in the daily 8:00 AM summary email |

Toggle any switch to enable or disable that channel for that notification type. Changes take effect immediately — no save button needed.

> [!TIP]
> If you find your inbox getting too many SWIMS emails, turn off **Email** for less critical notification types and rely on the **Digest** instead. You'll get one tidy summary each morning rather than individual emails throughout the day.

## Push Notifications

Push notifications require a one-time browser permission:

1. On the Notification Preferences page, look for the **Push Notifications** section.
2. Click **Enable Push Notifications**.
3. Your browser will show a permission prompt — click **Allow**.
4. Push notifications are now active for your current browser/device.

You can enable push notifications on multiple devices (work computer, phone, tablet) — each registers separately.

### Disabling Push

To disable push on a device:

1. Go to **Notification Preferences → Push Notifications**.
2. Click **Disable** for the device you want to remove.

Or revoke the permission in your browser's site settings.

## Installing SWIMS as an App (PWA)

SWIMS can be installed on your device as an app — this allows it to send push notifications even when you don't have a browser tab open.

- **On desktop (Chrome/Edge)**: look for the install icon in the browser address bar and click it.
- **On mobile**: use "Add to Home Screen" from your browser menu.

Once installed, SWIMS opens in its own window and behaves like a native app.
