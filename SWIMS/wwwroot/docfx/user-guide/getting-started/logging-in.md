# Logging In

## Accessing SWIMS

Open your web browser and navigate to the SWIMS URL provided by your administrator. SWIMS works best in **Google Chrome**, **Microsoft Edge**, or **Mozilla Firefox** on a desktop or laptop computer. It is also accessible on tablets.

## The Login Screen

You will be presented with a single login form. SWIMS supports two types of accounts:

- **Active Directory (AD) accounts** — your normal Windows/network username and password
- **Local SWIMS accounts** — a username and password created specifically for SWIMS

Both types use the same login form.

### Logging In with an AD Account

Enter your **short network username** (e.g. `jdoe`, not your full email address) and your **Windows password**, then click **Sign In**.

### Logging In with a Local Account

Enter your **email address** and **SWIMS password**, then click **Sign In**.

> [!TIP]
> Not sure which type of account you have? Try your short network username first. If it fails, try your email address.

## Two-Factor Authentication

If your account has **two-factor authentication (2FA)** enabled, after entering your password you will be redirected to a second screen asking for a 6-digit code from your authenticator app (e.g. Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator).

Enter the current 6-digit code and click **Verify**. Codes refresh every 30 seconds — if it doesn't work, wait for the next code to appear.

See [Two-Factor Authentication](two-factor.md) for how to set this up.

## First Login / Account Confirmation

If you are logging in for the first time with a local SWIMS account, you may need to confirm your email address first. Check your inbox for a confirmation email from SWIMS and click the link before attempting to log in.

## Forgotten Password

On the login screen, click **Forgot your password?** and enter your email address. You will receive a password reset link by email.

> [!NOTE]
> If you use an Active Directory (network) account, password resets must be handled by your IT department — the SWIMS password reset flow does not apply to AD accounts.

## Locked Out

After several failed login attempts, your account may be temporarily locked. Contact your SWIMS administrator to unlock it.

## Logging Out

Always log out when you are done:

1. Click your **name or avatar** in the top-right corner of the screen.
2. Click **Sign Out**.

> [!WARNING]
> Do not simply close the browser tab. Always use Sign Out to properly end your session, especially on shared or public computers.
